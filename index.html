About me

I am an astrophysicist currently working at Leiden Observatory with Professor A. Vidotto. Previously, I was a post-doctoral fellow at the Harvard & Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics working with Professor A. Dupree. I completed my PhD in 2022 at the Institut de Recherche en Astrophysique et Planétologie, located at the Observatoire Midi Pyrénée (OMP) in Toulouse, France

Before starting my PhD, I obtained a master's degree in fundamental physics from the Aix-Marseille University, with a specialization in particles and astroparticles, and a bachelor's degree in Chemistry from the University Toulon, France.

When I am not doing science, you will find me playing music, reading, fixing my bike or running.

Research

My research leverages the last generation of high-resolution spectrometers to study stars. I develop models and analyses for their characterization, and to study their magnetic fields.

⬘ I have relied on near-infrared spectroscopy to derive the properties of M dwarfs. These stars are the most numerous around us, and are key targets to study our local Universe and to search for habitable exoplanets.

⬗ I have developed new models to study magnetic fields in low-mass stars. Their study is essential to understand the processes at the origin of stellar magnetic fields and constrain stellar cycles.

⬙ I have leveraged multi-fiber spectroscopic observations, allowing us to record simultaneous spectra for dozens of stars in young globular clusters to study the properties of Be stars.
